# User Interviews & Support Tickets — Orbital Document Q&A Beta

*Collected over 3 weeks from ~50 beta users across 8 law firms. Names anonymised.*

---

> "Love this tool — we used it on a portfolio acquisition and it saved us hours on the initial
> review. But I had to re-upload the same lease agreement in three different chats because I
> was asking different types of questions about it each time. Feels like the tool should just
> remember my documents."
>
> — Senior Associate, Firm D

---

> "The speed is genuinely impressive when it works. But twice now it's given me an answer
> that sounds completely authoritative and is just... not in the document. That's terrifying when
> you're advising a client on a £40M acquisition."
>
> — Partner, Firm A

---

> "I'd love to be able to compare what two different documents say about the same topic —
> like pulling up the indemnity clause from both the purchase agreement and the lease side
> by side."
>
> — Associate, Firm F

---

> "My associate tried it for a week and then stopped. She got an answer that was completely
> fabricated — cited a clause that doesn't exist. She doesn't trust it now and I can't blame her.
> In our world, being confidently wrong is worse than being slow."
>
> — Partner, Firm B

---

> "Minor thing, but I find myself taking screenshots of the document viewer to highlight
> sections for my team. Would be nice to have actual annotation or highlighting."
>
> — Associate, Firm C

---

> "Could you add some kind of report export? I'm copy-pasting answers from the chat into
> a Word doc for the client, which defeats the purpose of saving time."
>
> — Senior Associate, Firm E

---

> "When the AI gives me an answer and actually tells me it's from section 4.2 of the lease,
> it's magic. Genuinely magic. When it doesn't cite anything specific, I have to go find it
> myself anyway, so what's the point?"
>
> — Associate, Firm A

---

> "Honestly? I'd pay double the licence fee if the AI would just tell me when it's not sure
> rather than making something up. A confidence indicator would change
> everything for me."
>
> — Partner, Firm A

---

> "The document viewer is functional but I miss being able to ctrl+F within it. I know exactly
> what clause I'm looking for sometimes and I just want to jump to it."
>
> — Trainee, Firm H
